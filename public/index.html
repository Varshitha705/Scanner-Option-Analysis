Options Alert System

Options Entry & Alert Analysis

For options, your entry and alert level needs two parts: one for the stock price and one for momentum confirmation. This helps you avoid entering too early and watching the candle go against you.

1. Alert Level = “Get Ready” Zone

Set this 0.2%–0.5% below your actual breakout level.

Example: If stock is squeezing under 15M resistance at $100.50, set alert around $100.30.

This gives you time to check volume and MACD before the candle prints. Do not enter here yet.

2. Entry Level = “Go” Trigger

Only enter when price and momentum both confirm. All three must happen together:

  • Price: 5M candle closes above resistance and VWAP.
  • Volume: 5M candle volume is more than 2x the average of the last 10 candles.
  • MACD 8,17,9: MACD line crosses above signal and histogram flips green.

For options, enter right after the 5M candle closes to avoid fake wicks.

3. Where NOT to Enter

  • Do not enter just because price is above 9/21 EMA.
  • Do not enter when POC is still above price.
  • Do not enter on tiny-volume green candles.

Quick 15M Big-Move Setup

  • Draw level: Last 15M swing high + VWAP = breakout line.
  • Alert 1: Price touches breakout line -1% → check screen.
  • Alert 2: 5M volume spike 2x → watch MACD.
  • Entry: 5M close above level + MACD cross → buy option.
Options tip: Enter 5–10 minutes after the big candle starts, not during the wick. IV can spike during the candle, so waiting one candle often gives better confirmation and fill. Big candle chasing is high risk because theta decay and IV crush can hurt even when direction is right. Only risk what you can afford to lose and always use stops.
